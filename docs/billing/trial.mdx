## Free trial

Every new account starts with a 30-day free trial. No credit card is required.

| Included | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Storage | 1 TB (1,024 GB) |
| Egress bandwidth | 2 TB |
| API operations | Unlimited |
| Object Lock | Fully available (30-day max retention during trial) |
| Duration | 30 days from account creation |

The trial gives you enough capacity to test real workloads, not just sample uploads. All features are available during the trial, including Object Lock with Compliance mode.

## What happens when the trial ends

| Phase | Days | What you can do |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| **Active trial** | 0-30 | Full access. Upload, download, manage data. |
| **Grace period** | 31-37 | **Read-only.** Download your data and upgrade to a paid plan. No new uploads. |
| **Expired** | 38+ | Account closed. All data permanently deleted. API keys disabled. |

You will receive email reminders at:
- **Day 15** -- Halfway through your trial, with an upgrade link.
- **Day 25** -- 5 days left, with an upgrade link.
- **Day 31** -- Trial ended, grace period started.
- **Day 36** -- Data deletion in 48 hours.
- **Day 38** -- Deletion confirmed.

:::note
You can upgrade to a paid plan at any time during the trial or grace period. Upgrading restores full access immediately.
:::

Pricing is usage-based. You pay for what you store, measured as the average storage consumed across the billing period. There are no minimum commitments.

## Payment

Payments are processed through Stripe. You can add a credit card from **Settings > Billing** in the dashboard.

- Invoices are generated monthly.
- Usage details are visible in the dashboard under **Billing > Usage**.
- Payment is due on receipt of invoice.

## Cancellation

You can cancel your paid plan at any time from the dashboard. After cancellation:

1. Your account remains active through the end of the current billing period.
2. At the end of the billing period, the account transitions to read-only.
3. After a grace period, data is permanently deleted.

If you have objects with active Object Lock retention, those objects cannot be deleted until retention expires, even after cancellation.
